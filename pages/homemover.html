Type of Transport (Size and Weight)

While transporting a mobile home, it greatly impacts shipping prices. Different types of mobile homes differ in weight and size, which summarizes the total shipping cost. Generally, these fall into three major classes; single, double, and triple-wide mobile home or multi-wide.

1
Single-Wide Home
Single wide is a small-size manufactured residence that has an average covered area of 500 sqft-1178 sqft. Its width is nearly 14 feet to 18 feet and its length lies between 37 to 76 feet. Due to less weight (25,000lbs. to 40,000lbs. or more), small dimensions, and easy to disassemble and assemble, these can easily move at an affordable rate without any delay. The cost to ship a Single-wide mobile home ranges from $950 to $7,500.
2
Double Wide
Double-wide homes have an area of 700 sqft to 1800 sqft, their width lies between 27 feet to 36 feet, and the length ranges between 42 feet to 76 feet. Their minimum weight is 30,000 lbs. and the maximum weight is 60,000 lbs. As compared to single wide, disassembling these homes is quite difficult and requires more time and extra labor, which costs higher. An estimated cost of moving a double-wide home is $1,800 to $13,500.
3
Multi-Wide Or Triple-Wide
Multi-wide or triple-wide manufactured homes typically exceed 43 feet in width and can be up to 70 feet or more in length. These homes are the most expensive mobile homes. Their assembly and disassembly are much more difficult and time-consuming than single-wide and double-wide homes, and their weight exceeds 80,000 lbs. The cost to ship this oversized and heavy property ranges between $5,000 to $19,000 or more.
